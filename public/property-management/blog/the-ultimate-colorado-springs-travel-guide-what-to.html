Nestled at the foot of the majestic Pikes Peak, Colorado Springs is a city that effortlessly blends rugged outdoor adventure with rich history and a vibrant cultural scene. Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie looking to summit a “fourteener,” a history buff eager to walk the streets of a Wild West town, or a family searching for unforgettable memories, this city has a unique magic that captivates every visitor.

As locals who’ve explored every trail and tasted our way through the city’s best eateries, we’ve put together the ultimate guide to help you plan the perfect getaway. Experience Breathtaking Natural Wonders

Colorado Springs is an outdoor lover’s paradise, defined by its dramatic landscapes and iconic geological formations. You can’t visit without immersing yourself in the stunning scenery.

Garden of the Gods Park

This National Natural Landmark is non-negotiable—it’s the crown jewel of Colorado Springs. Imagine towering red rock formations, some over 300 million years old, jutting into a brilliant blue sky. It’s a photographer’s dream and a nature lover’s playground.

  • What to Do: You can drive the scenic loop, but the best way to experience the park is on foot. The Perkins Central Garden Trail is a 1.5-mile paved loop that’s wheelchair and stroller-accessible, winding you through the heart of the most famous formations like Kissing Camels and Sentinel Spires. For more of a hike, explore the myriad of interconnected trails that offer different perspectives. Rock climbing is also popular here, but requires a permit.
  • Insider Tip: The park is free to enter! To avoid the biggest crowds, go on a weekday morning or late in the afternoon for golden hour, when the setting sun makes the red rocks glow.
  • Address: 1805 N 30th St, Colorado Springs, CO 80904

Pikes Peak - America’s Mountain

The very inspiration for the song “America the Beautiful,” Pikes Peak looms over the city at a staggering 14,115 feet. Reaching the summit is a quintessential Colorado Springs experience, offering panoramic views that will leave you breathless (literally—the altitude is no joke!).

  • How to Get to the Top:
    • Pikes Peak Highway: A 19-mile scenic toll road that you can drive yourself. Allow at least 2-3 hours for the round trip, not including time at the summit.
    • The Broadmoor Manitou and Pikes Peak Cog Railway: A historic and relaxing way to ascend. This 9-mile journey takes you through stunning forests and above the treeline, all while you sit back and enjoy the view. Book tickets well in advance!
    • Hike It: For the truly adventurous, the 13-mile Barr Trail is a challenging trek that gains 7,800 feet in elevation. This is a full-day, strenuous hike that requires preparation.
  • Insider Tip: No matter how you get there, be sure to grab a world-famous high-altitude donut from the Summit Visitor Center. They’re made with a special recipe that allows them to puff up perfectly at 14,115 feet!

The Broadmoor Seven Falls

A series of seven cascading waterfalls tumbling 181 feet down a granite box canyon, Seven Falls is a sight to behold. It’s a privately owned attraction, so there is an admission fee, but the stunning scenery is well worth it.

  • What to Do: Climb the 224 steep steps alongside the falls for incredible views from the top. For those who prefer a less strenuous option, an in-mountain elevator whisks you up to the “Eagle’s Nest” viewing platform. Trails at the top lead to Midnight Falls and Inspiration Point.
  • Insider Tip: The falls are illuminated by colored lights after dark, creating a magical, must-see spectacle. Check their schedule for evening hours.
  • Address: 1045 Lower Gold Camp Rd, Colorado Springs, CO 80905

Embark on an Outdoor Adventure

Beyond the big-name attractions, the Springs is crisscrossed with trails and opportunities for adventure.

Hiking and Biking Trails

  • Red Rock Canyon Open Space: Often called the “local’s Garden of the Gods,” this park offers a similar red rock landscape with fewer crowds and more dog-friendly trails. The Contemplative Trail is a local favorite for a moderately easy hike.
  • Palmer Park: An urban oasis with a network of trails for all skill levels. The high points in the park offer fantastic 360-degree views of the city and Pikes Peak.
  • Manitou Incline: Not for the faint of heart! This trail gains nearly 2,000 feet of elevation in just under a mile. It’s a grueling workout beloved by athletes, but the view from the top is your reward. Reservations are now required, so plan ahead.

Ziplining and Rock Climbing

  • Adventures Out West: Offers thrilling zipline tours right at the base of Pikes Peak, giving you a bird’s-eye view of the stunning landscape.
  • Front Range Climbing Company: Whether you’re a seasoned climber or a complete beginner, they offer guided trips in Garden of the Gods and other spectacular local climbing spots.

Step Back in Time: Historic & Cultural Hotspots

Colorado Springs has a rich and varied history, from its Wild West roots to its modern identity as a hub for Olympic athletes.

Old Colorado City

Step onto the historic streets of Old Colorado City, the original capital of the Colorado Territory. Today, this charming district is lined with Victorian architecture housing art galleries, unique boutiques, and fantastic restaurants. It’s the perfect place to spend an afternoon strolling, shopping for souvenirs, and soaking up the historic atmosphere.

Manitou Springs

Just a few minutes’ drive from Colorado Springs, the quirky, artistic town of Manitou Springs feels like a world away. Famous for its eight naturally carbonated mineral springs, you can take a walking tour and taste the unique waters from each one. The town is also home to a classic penny arcade, eclectic shops, and a vibrant arts scene.

Exploring these historic neighborhoods gives you a real feel for the area’s character. After a long day of walking through art galleries in Old Colorado City and tasting the mineral waters of Manitou, having a comfortable home base to return to is key. For larger families or groups, finding a place that combines comfort with a prime location is essential.

For groups of up to 12, the Pikes Peak View Lodge is an incredible option. Located just minutes from Old Colorado City, it offers spacious living areas, a game room for the kids, and a sprawling deck with—you guessed it—unforgettable views of Pikes Peak. It’s the perfect spot to unwind and share stories from your day’s adventures.

U.S. Olympic & Paralympic Museum

A modern architectural marvel, this 60,000-square-foot museum is a must-visit. It’s a state-of-the-art tribute to the athletes of Team USA. The interactive exhibits are incredibly engaging, allowing you to try out a virtual ski jump or race against an Olympian. It’s an inspiring experience for all ages.

  • Address: 200 S Sierra Madre St, Colorado Springs, CO 80903

Unforgettable Family-Friendly Fun

Traveling with kids? Colorado Springs is packed with attractions that will delight the whole family.

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo

America’s only mountain zoo, Cheyenne Mountain Zoo offers a unique experience. The enclosures are built into the mountainside, providing incredible views of the city below. The highlight for many is hand-feeding the massive giraffe herd—an experience your kids will never forget.

Cave of the Winds Mountain Park

Explore the mysteries underground on a guided cave tour. The Discovery Tour is a great 45-minute introduction for all ages. For more adventurous families, the Lantern Tour offers a spookier, historical perspective. Above ground, the park features thrill rides like the Terror-dactyl, which launches you into the canyon at nearly 100 mph.

The North Pole - Home of Santa’s Workshop

A vintage Christmas-themed amusement park that has been delighting families since 1956. With rides geared toward younger children, shows, and the chance to meet Santa himself (even in July), it’s a whimsical and nostalgic day out.

A Taste of the Springs: Where to Eat & Drink

After all that exploring, you’re going to be hungry. The Colorado Springs food scene has exploded in recent years, offering everything from rustic breweries to upscale dining.

Local Breweries

  • Bristol Brewing Company: Housed in a former elementary school (Ivywild School), Bristol is a local institution. Grab a pint of their Laughing Lab Scottish Ale and enjoy the lively atmosphere.
  • Phantom Canyon Brewing Co.: The city’s oldest brewery, located in a historic downtown building. They offer a great food menu to go with their wide selection of craft beers.

Must-Try Restaurants

  • The Rabbit Hole: For a unique dining experience, head to this “Alice in Wonderland” themed spot located underground. The inventive menu and whimsical cocktails are fantastic. Reservations are highly recommended.
  • Shuga’s: Known for its cozy, eclectic vibe and delicious “Shuga’s Famous Spicy Shrimp Soup.” It’s a perfect spot for lunch or a casual dinner.
  • Paravicini’s Italian Bistro: A lively, family-owned restaurant in Old Colorado City serving up classic, hearty Italian dishes. As seen on “Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives”!

Coffee & Cafes

  • The Wild Goose Meeting House: A popular spot for coffee, breakfast, or a light lunch. They also have a great selection of beer and wine for later in the day.
  • Story Coffee Company: A tiny, beautifully designed coffee shop focused on creating a simple, perfect cup of coffee. It’s a true local gem.

A Guide to the Seasons in Colorado Springs

The “best” time to visit really depends on what you want to do.

Spring (March - May)

Weather can be unpredictable—snow one day, 70 degrees the next! But it’s a beautiful time as the city starts to come to life. Trails are less crowded, and you can often find better deals on accommodations.

Summer (June - August)

Peak season for a reason. The weather is warm and sunny, perfect for hiking, biking, and all outdoor activities. This is the busiest time, so be sure to book accommodations and popular attractions well in advance. Afternoon thunderstorms are common, so plan your high-elevation activities for the morning.

Fall (September - October)

Arguably the most beautiful season. The crowds thin out, the weather is crisp and pleasant, and the changing aspen leaves in the mountains create a stunning golden landscape. It’s an ideal time for hiking and scenic drives.

Winter (November - February)

Colorado Springs gets plenty of sunshine even in winter, and snow often melts quickly in the city. It’s a quieter, more peaceful time to visit. While high-elevation trails may be inaccessible, lower-elevation parks like Garden of the Gods are beautiful with a dusting of snow. Plus, you’re only a couple of hours’ drive from world-class ski resorts.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time of year to visit Colorado Springs?

For warm weather and access to all attractions, June through September is ideal. However, for fewer crowds, beautiful fall colors, and pleasant hiking weather, September and October are fantastic.

How many days do you need to see Colorado Springs?

We recommend at least three to four full days. This gives you enough time to see the main highlights like Pikes Peak and Garden of the Gods, explore a historic district like Old Colorado City or Manitou Springs, and fit in a hike or another family-friendly activity without feeling rushed.

Is Colorado Springs expensive to visit?

Colorado Springs can be very budget-friendly. Many of its best attractions, like Garden of the Gods and Red Rock Canyon Open Space, are completely free. Choosing a vacation rental with a kitchen can also save you a lot of money on dining out.

What is Colorado Springs most known for?

Colorado Springs is most famous for Pikes Peak (“America’s Mountain”) and the stunning red rock formations of Garden of the Gods Park. It is also known as “Olympic City USA,” as it is home to the U.S. Olympic & Paralympic Training Center and Museum.
