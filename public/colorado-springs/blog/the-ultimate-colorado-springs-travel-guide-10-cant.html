The Ultimate Colorado Springs Travel Guide: 10 Can't-Miss Experiences

2026-04-13 • 9 min read
Home / Colorado Springs Stays / The Ultimate Colorado Springs Travel Guide: 10 Can't-Miss Experiences

Forget the generic travel blogs that list the same three attractions. You’re here for the real deal—the stuff that makes you feel like you’ve discovered the city’s secrets. Think of this as your inside track to Colorado Springs, a guide to experiencing the city beyond the surface. We’re going to skip the fluff and get right to the must-do, can’t-miss adventures.

1. Hike Through Garden of the Gods (The Right Way)

Okay, let’s get the obvious one out of the way first. You can’t come to Colorado Springs and not see Garden of the Gods. But most people just drive the loop, snap a picture of Balanced Rock, and leave. You’re not going to do that.

Why It’s a Must-Do

These magnificent red rock formations jutting out of the earth with Pikes Peak in the background are genuinely breathtaking. It’s a National Natural Landmark for a reason. The paved Central Garden Trail is accessible and gets you up close to the main formations like Kissing Camels and Sentinel Spires.

The Local’s Tip

To truly experience the park, you need to get on the dirt trails. The secret to avoiding the crowds is to park at the Trading Post and take the Palmer Trail. It’s a slightly more moderate loop that offers incredible, wide-open views of the entire park without the bumper-to-bumper foot traffic. Go at sunrise or an hour before sunset for the best light—the rocks practically glow.

2. Conquer Pikes Peak (Or at Least the Donuts)

America’s Mountain. The inspiration for “America the Beautiful.” You have two main options to get to the 14,115-foot summit: drive the winding Pikes Peak Highway or take the Broadmoor’s Pikes Peak Cog Railway.

Why It’s a Must-Do

The views from the top are otherworldly. On a clear day, you can see five states. The brand new Summit Visitor Center is a destination in itself, but the real rite of passage is eating one of their world-famous high-altitude donuts.

The Local’s Tip

The Cog Railway is 100% the better experience. You get all the views without the white-knuckle stress of driving the switchbacks. But you absolutely must book your tickets weeks, if not months, in advance, especially for a summer trip. They sell out fast. If you do drive, go early to get a parking spot at the top and give your car’s brakes a break by using a low gear on the way down.

3. Test Your Legs on the Manitou Incline

This is not a hike. This is a challenge. The Incline is a set of old railroad ties going straight up the side of a mountain—gaining 2,000 feet of elevation in just under a mile. It is famously, brutally difficult.

Why It’s a Must-Do

The bragging rights are immense. The sense of accomplishment at the top is unbeatable, and the views are a fantastic reward for your burning legs. It’s a true Colorado fitness test.

The Local’s Tip

Do not underestimate this climb. Bring at least a liter of water per person. You now need a reservation, so book it online for free beforehand. And please, for the love of all that is holy, do not go back down the Incline. Take the much gentler, four-mile Barr Trail down. It’s a lovely, shaded walk that will give your knees a chance to recover.

4. Wander Through Historic Old Colorado City

Before Colorado Springs was a city, Old Colorado City was its own town—the first capital of the Colorado Territory, in fact. Today, it’s a charming historic district lined with Victorian buildings housing art galleries, indie boutiques, and fantastic restaurants.

Why It’s a Must-Do

It’s the perfect place to spend a relaxed afternoon. You can find unique souvenirs, incredible art, and some of the best food in the city, all within a few walkable blocks.

The Local’s Tip

Skip the chain restaurants and head straight to Paravicini’s Italian Bistro for dinner. It’s loud, authentic, and absolutely delicious. My go-to is the Veal Saltimbocca. For a treat, stop by the Rocky Mountain Chocolate Factory for a caramel apple the size of your head.

5. Explore Funky Manitou Springs

Nestled at the base of Pikes Peak, Manitou Springs is Colorado Springs’ quirky, bohemian little sister. The downtown is packed with art galleries, psychic readers, and old-school arcades.

Why It’s a Must-Do

The town is built around eight natural mineral springs, each with a unique taste and mineral content. You can walk around town with a cup and sample them all for free. It’s a unique experience you won’t find anywhere else.

The Local’s Tip

The Penny Arcade is a must-visit. It’s a sprawling collection of vintage arcade games (some are still a nickel!) and classic skeeball. Bring a roll of quarters and lose track of time. Afterwards, grab a beer and a burger at the Manitou Brewing Company.

6. Hand-Feed Giraffes at Cheyenne Mountain Zoo

This isn’t just any zoo. Built on the side of a mountain, it’s America’s only mountain zoo. Getting from exhibit to exhibit is a workout, but the views of the city below are worth it.

Why It’s a Must-Do

The giraffe herd. It’s one of the largest in the country, and you can buy lettuce to hand-feed them at eye level. Looking a full-grown giraffe in the eye as it gently takes food from your hand is an unforgettable core memory.

The Local’s Tip

Buy your tickets online in advance for a specific time slot. Go in the morning when the animals are most active and the weather is cooler. Wear comfortable shoes—you’ll be walking up and down some serious hills.

7. Chase Waterfalls at The Broadmoor Seven Falls

A series of seven cascading waterfalls tumbling 181 feet down a box canyon. It’s a spectacular sight, and you can get right up close.

Why It’s a Must-Do

The scale of the falls is impressive. You can climb the 224 steep steps alongside the falls for incredible views or take the in-mountain elevator to the “Eagle’s Nest” overlook.

The Local’s Tip

The falls are beautiful during the day, but they’re magical at night when they’re illuminated with colored lights. It’s a completely different and less crowded experience. Check their seasonal schedule for nighttime hours.

8. Get Inspired at the U.S. Olympic & Paralympic Museum

This is one of the newest and most impressive additions to downtown Colorado Springs. The museum is a stunning piece of architecture and a highly interactive tribute to Team USA.

Why It’s a Must-Do

Even if you’re not a huge sports fan, the stories of perseverance and dedication are incredibly moving. The interactive exhibits let you try your hand at everything from alpine skiing to a 30-meter dash against an Olympian.

The Local’s Tip

Start your visit on the top floor and work your way down the spiraling ramps. The flow of the museum is designed this way and tells a much better story. Give yourself at least two to three hours here; there’s a lot to see and do.

9. Find Solitude at Red Rock Canyon Open Space

Think of this as Garden of the Gods’ less-famous, more-relaxed cousin. It’s a former quarry that has been transformed into a 1,474-acre park with soaring red rock formations, hiking trails, and even a few small lakes.

Why It’s a Must-Do

It offers a similar landscape to Garden of the Gods but with a fraction of the crowds. It’s also extremely dog-friendly (including off-leash areas), making it a favorite spot for anyone traveling with a four-legged friend.

The Local’s Tip

The Contemplative Trail to the Upper Hogback gives you a panoramic view that rivals anything in the more popular park next door. It’s the perfect spot to watch the sunset over the mountains.

10. Grab a Beer at an Old Elementary School

Yes, you read that right. The Ivywild School is a beautifully restored 1916 elementary school that now houses a community marketplace, including a brewery, a distillery, a coffee shop, and a deli.

Why It’s a Must-Do

It’s the ultimate hyper-local experience. You can sip a craft beer from Bristol Brewing Company in what used to be a classroom, or enjoy a cocktail from Axe and the Oak Distillery in the former principal’s office.

The Local’s Tip

This is the perfect final stop of your trip. The atmosphere is laid-back and friendly. My absolute favorite thing to order is a pint of Bristol’s Laughing Lab Scottish Ale. Grab a seat on the patio and toast to an amazing Colorado Springs adventure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best month to visit Colorado Springs?

While summer is the most popular, the best month to visit is September. The summer crowds have thinned out, the weather is still warm and pleasant, and the fall colors are just beginning to appear on the mountainsides.

How many days do you need in Colorado Springs?

To comfortably experience the main highlights without feeling rushed, plan for at least 3 to 4 full days. This gives you enough time for Pikes Peak, Garden of the Gods, and a few other attractions on this list.

Do I need to worry about altitude sickness?

Yes, you should be mindful of it. Colorado Springs sits at over 6,000 feet, and many attractions are much higher. The key is to acclimate. Take it easy on your first day, avoid strenuous activity, and drink way more water than you think you need.
