Best Seafood Restaurants in Panama City Beach: A Local's Guide

2026-04-11 • 12 min read
Home / Panama City Beach Stays / Best Seafood Restaurants in Panama City Beach: A Local's Guide

Panama City Beach, with its sugar-white sands and emerald-green waters, isn’t just a paradise for sunbathers—it’s a haven for seafood lovers. The Gulf of Mexico provides an incredible bounty, and local chefs transform this daily catch into culinary masterpieces. But with so many options lining Front Beach Road, how do you separate the tourist traps from the truly unforgettable dining experiences?

That’s where we come in. Forget the generic lists. We’ve talked to the locals, sampled the chowders, and shucked the oysters to bring you an insider’s guide to the best seafood in PCB. Whether you’re looking for a rustic, come-as-you-are oyster bar or an elegant waterfront spot for a special occasion, this is your definitive map to the best Panama City Beach dining.

Captain Anderson’s Restaurant: A Waterfront Classic

Address: 5551 N Lagoon Dr, Panama City, FL 32408

You can’t talk about local fresh seafood in Panama City Beach without mentioning Captain Anderson’s. Since 1967, this sprawling, waterfront restaurant has been a cornerstone of the local dining scene. It’s not just a restaurant; it’s an institution. Situated on the Grand Lagoon, you can watch the fleet unload their daily catch right on the docks as you wait for your table.

The Vibe

Captain Anderson’s is old-school Florida at its finest. It’s a bustling, family-friendly spot that can seat hundreds, yet it maintains a classic, maritime charm. The decor features rich woods, nautical artifacts, and large windows offering panoramic views of the marina. It gets busy, especially in peak season, so plan to arrive early or enjoy a drink at the bar.

Must-Try Dishes

While the menu is extensive, the star of the show is the “Famous Grilled Bay Shrimp.” These aren’t your average shrimp; they’re large, fresh, and grilled to perfection in a secret blend of spices. Other can’t-miss items include the She-Crab Soup, the Scamp Imperial (a local fish specialty), and any of their fresh-off-the-boat grilled fish entrees. Their early-bird specials are legendary for a reason, offering great value for top-quality food.

Local’s Tip

The restaurant doesn’t take reservations, so the wait can be long. The secret is to go right when they open (around 4:30 PM) to get your name on the list. Or, do what the locals do: grab a cocktail and some appetizers at the bar while you wait. It’s an experience in itself.

Dusty’s Oyster Bar: The Quintessential Dive

Address: 16450 Front Beach Rd, Panama City Beach, FL 32413

If you’re looking for white tablecloths and fancy decor, keep walking. If you’re looking for ice-cold beer, incredible oysters, and an authentic beach dive atmosphere, you’ve found your mecca. Dusty’s is a PCB legend, famous for its dollar-bill-covered walls and a no-frills attitude that puts the focus squarely on the food.

The Vibe

It’s loud, it’s crowded, and it’s unapologetically a dive bar. The walls and ceiling are plastered with signed dollar bills from decades of happy customers. You’ll find a mix of bikers, families, and locals bellied up to the bar, all there for the same reason: amazing, fresh seafood without the pretense.

Must-Try Dishes

Oysters are the main event here. Get them raw on the half-shell, steamed, or try their famous baked oysters topped with jalapeños and cheese. The Jalapeño Bacon-Wrapped Grouper is another crowd-pleaser, perfectly combining smoky, spicy, and fresh flavors. Don’t sleep on the Fried Crab Claws or the hearty Seafood Gumbo.

Local’s Tip

Dusty’s is small and always packed. Your best bet is to go for a late lunch or an early dinner on a weekday to avoid the biggest crowds. And be sure to bring a signed dollar bill to add to the collection!

Schooners Last Local Beach Club: Sunset Views and Great Food

Address: 5121 Gulf Dr, Panama City, FL 32408

Perched directly on the sand on the historic east end of PCB, Schooners bills itself as “the last local beach club,” and it lives up to the name. It’s the ultimate beach-day dining spot where you can walk straight from the surf to your table.

The Vibe

Barefoot-friendly and ultra-casual. The open-air dining room and expansive beach deck provide unbeatable views of the Gulf. It’s laid-back during the day and transforms into a lively party spot at night with live music. The daily sunset celebration, where they fire a cannon to mark the moment, is a can’t-miss PCB tradition.

Must-Try Dishes

The menu is a mix of beach bar classics and excellent seafood entrees. The Seared Crab Cakes are packed with lump crab meat and very little filler. The Grouper Sandwich, served grilled, blackened, or fried, is one of the best on the beach. For a true taste of the Gulf, try the Tuna Medallions or the Seafood Platter.

Local’s Tip

Time your visit for the sunset cannon blast. It happens every day at dusk. Arrive about an hour beforehand to snag a prime table on the deck, order a round of drinks, and enjoy one of the best views in all of Panama City Beach.

The Grand Marlin: Upscale Coastal Cuisine

Address: 5323 N Lagoon Dr, Panama City Beach, FL 32408

Located right next to the Lighthouse Marina and overlooking the Grand Lagoon, The Grand Marlin offers a more refined seafood experience. It’s the perfect spot for a date night, a celebration, or when you just want to treat yourself to some of the highest-quality seafood in town.

The Vibe

Coastal elegance meets relaxed comfort. The main dining room is bright and airy with stunning water views, while the North Drop Bar offers a more casual, open-air setting. The service is top-notch, attentive without being stuffy. It strikes a beautiful balance between a special occasion restaurant and a comfortable local hangout.

Must-Try Dishes

The menu is printed daily to reflect the freshest catches. Start with the Lobster Fingers or the Smoked Tuna Dip. For your main, you can’t go wrong with the pristine fresh-catch selection, prepared grilled, blackened, or sautéed. A signature dish is the Grouper Piccata—a delicate, perfectly cooked fillet in a zesty lemon-caper butter sauce. Their raw bar is also exceptional, featuring a rotating selection of fresh oysters from across the country.

Local’s Tip

Make a reservation, especially for a weekend dinner. Request a table by the window for the best views of the marina. For a more casual experience, head upstairs to the North Drop Bar for happy hour, which features great drink specials and a killer view.

Firefly: A Surprising Seafood Star

Address: 535 Richard Jackson Blvd, Panama City Beach, FL 32407

You might not expect to find one of the best seafood restaurants tucked away in a shopping plaza, but Firefly is full of surprises. Known for its stunning main dining room with a massive, illuminated tree at its center, Firefly offers an atmosphere and a menu that rival any big-city fine dining establishment.

The Vibe

Magical and romantic. The “tree” in the main dining room creates an enchanting, al fresco-like ambiance indoors. There’s also a more casual library-themed bar and a sushi bar. It’s an ideal choice for anniversaries, birthdays, or any special evening out. While it’s upscale, it’s still the beach, so the dress code is more “resort casual” than formal.

Must-Try Dishes

While they’re famous for their steaks and sushi, the seafood is truly exceptional. The She-Crab Soup is rich, creamy, and legendary among locals. For an entree, the Pan-Seared Grouper with wasabi mashed potatoes is a flavor explosion. Their sushi is also some of the freshest and most creative in PCB, making it a great place to start your meal.

Local’s Tip

Firefly is located just a short drive from Pier Park, making it a perfect spot for dinner after a day of shopping or entertainment. Take advantage of their excellent happy hour, which includes specials on cocktails, wine, and sushi in the bar area.

Runaway Island: Beachfront Fun for the Whole Family

Address: 14521 Front Beach Rd, Panama City Beach, FL 32413

If you’re traveling with a crew and need a place that satisfies everyone from toddlers to grandparents, Runaway Island is a fantastic choice. With a massive dining area, a huge beachfront deck, and direct beach access, it combines good food with a fun, family-friendly atmosphere.

The Vibe

Lively, casual, and built for fun. The main draw is the expansive, multi-level deck right on the sand. There’s plenty of space for kids to play on the beach while parents relax with a drink. It’s a high-energy spot with live music and a vacation vibe that’s impossible to resist.

Must-Try Dishes

The menu is approachable and has something for everyone. The Grouper Cheeks appetizer is a unique and delicious local treat. For entrees, the Blackened Redfish topped with a shrimp cream sauce is a standout. They also serve excellent fish tacos and a massive fried seafood platter if you’re feeling extra hungry.

Local’s Tip

Runaway Island has its own private boardwalk to the beach. It’s the perfect place to let the kids burn off some energy before or after the meal. Parking can be tight, but they offer a large overflow lot across the street.

Anderson Seafood Market: The Freshest Catch to Cook at Home

Address: 5551 N Lagoon Dr, Panama City, FL 32408

Sometimes, the best seafood meal is the one you make yourself. Located right next to the famous restaurant, Anderson Seafood Market is where the locals go to buy the same fresh, gulf-to-table seafood served in the city’s best restaurants.

The Vibe

It’s a classic, no-frills seafood market. The smell of fresh, salty seafood hits you the moment you walk in. The staff are incredibly knowledgeable and can tell you what just came off the boats. You’ll find everything from whole red snapper and grouper fillets to Apalachicola oysters, royal red shrimp, and homemade smoked tuna dip.

What to Get

Ask what’s freshest—it changes daily! Royal Red Shrimp are a deep-water delicacy that taste like a cross between shrimp and lobster. Pick up some fresh grouper or snapper fillets for the grill. Don’t leave without a container of their legendary Smoked Tuna Dip and some cocktail sauce.

Local’s Tip

They’ll steam your shrimp for you on the spot with their signature seasoning! It’s the perfect, easy meal to bring back to your vacation rental. Just grab some lemons and a loaf of crusty bread, and dinner is served. Frequently Asked Questions

What kind of seafood is Panama City Beach known for?

Panama City Beach is famous for its incredible Gulf seafood. The most popular local catches include Grouper (especially Scamp Grouper), Red Snapper, Amberjack, and Mahi-Mahi. You’ll also find an abundance of delicious Gulf Shrimp, Blue Crabs, and, of course, world-famous Apalachicola Bay Oysters.

What is the most famous seafood restaurant in Panama City Beach?

While there are many beloved spots, Captain Anderson’s Restaurant is arguably the most famous and iconic. It has been a PCB institution since 1967, known for its waterfront views of the fishing fleet and classic preparations of local fresh seafood.

Can you buy fresh seafood to cook yourself in PCB?

Absolutely! The best place to buy fresh, off-the-boat seafood is a local market like Anderson Seafood Market or Buddy’s Seafood Market. You can buy whole fish, fillets, shrimp, oysters, and more to cook back at your vacation rental. Many will even steam your shrimp for you free of charge.

Are there any budget-friendly seafood options in Panama City Beach?

Yes! You don’t have to break the bank to enjoy the best seafood in PCB. Places like Dusty’s Oyster Bar offer excellent value, especially on their raw and baked oysters. Many restaurants also have fantastic lunch specials or early-bird menus that provide a great meal at a lower price point. Grabbing a delicious Grouper Sandwich from a casual spot like Schooners is another great budget-friendly choice.

From legendary waterfront institutions to casual beachside shacks, the Panama City Beach dining scene is a seafood lover’s dream. Every meal is an opportunity to taste the incredible bounty of the Gulf of Mexico, prepared with local pride and expertise.
