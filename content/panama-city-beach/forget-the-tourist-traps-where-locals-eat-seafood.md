## The Classic Waterfront Experience (That’s Actually Worth It)

### Captain Anderson's Restaurant & Waterfront Market

Yes, it's famous. Yes, there's always a line. And yes, it is 100% worth it. Captain Anderson's isn't just a restaurant; it's a Panama City Beach institution that has been serving phenomenal seafood since 1967. The key is knowing how to do it right.

![fishing boats docked at a marina at sunset in Florida](/static/images/blog/panama-city-beach/fishing-boats-docked-at-a-marina-at-sunset-in-flor-59d5e0.jpg)

*   **Why Locals Love It**: Consistency and quality. While many "famous" spots rest on their laurels, Captain Anderson's still delivers. The main dining room overlooks the Grand Lagoon, and if you time it right, you can watch their own fishing fleet unload the day's catch right at the docks. It's dinner and a show. The attached waterfront market is also one of the best in the area for fresh, just-off-the-boat fish.
*   **What to Order**: You can't go wrong, but the "Famous" Grilled Bay Shrimp is a classic for a reason. For a true taste of the Gulf, get the Scamp Imperial—a delicate local fish baked with a rich crabmeat stuffing. The She-Crab soup is legendary, and their Greek-style preparations (a nod to the family's heritage) are unique and delicious.
*   **Pro Tip**: The doors open at 4:30 PM. Be there by 4:15 PM, especially during peak season (March-August), if you don't want to wait an hour or more. Ask for a table by the window to get the best view of the fishing boats.
*   **Address**: [5551 N Lagoon Dr, Panama City, FL 32408](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=5551+N+Lagoon+Dr,+Panama+City,+FL+32408)

## The Hidden Gem with a Cult Following

### J. Michael's Restaurant and Bar

From the outside, J. Michael's looks like an unassuming, slightly weathered pub on a busy stretch of Thomas Drive. Don't let that fool you. Inside, you'll find some of the most meticulously prepared and creative seafood in town. It's a true "if you know, you know" spot.

*   **Why Locals Love It**: It's the antithesis of a tourist trap. The vibe is laid-back and unpretentious, but the food is serious. It's where local chefs and service industry folks often go on their nights off. You won't find flashy decor, just a cozy, dark-wood bar and dining room filled with people who are there for one thing: incredible food.
*   **What to Order**: The baked oysters are a must. They have a dozen different preparations, but the "Oysters J. Michael" (topped with shrimp, bacon, spinach, and a three-cheese blend) are life-changing. Their grouper, whether you get it grilled, fried, or blackened, is always perfectly cooked. For something different, try the Tuna Poke Nachos.
*   **Pro Tip**: It's small and doesn't take reservations, so expect a wait. Put your name on the list and grab a drink at the bar. The bartenders are fantastic and it's part of the experience. Don't be in a rush; good things come to those who wait here.
*   **Address**: [3210 Thomas Dr, Panama City, FL 32408](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=3210+Thomas+Dr,+Panama+City,+FL+32408)

## The "Flip-Flops Required" Beach Shack

### Dusty's Oyster Bar

If you're looking for white tablecloths, keep walking. If you want ice-cold beer, friendly service, and some of the best oysters on the Gulf Coast served in a place plastered with signed dollar bills, you've found your mecca. Dusty's is a rite of passage in PCB.



*   **Why Locals Love It**: It's authentically, unapologetically itself. It's loud, it's crowded, and it's been the same for decades—in the best way possible. This is the ultimate come-as-you-are joint. You'll see salty fishermen sitting next to visiting families, all chowing down on fresh seafood and soaking in the dive-bar atmosphere.
*   **What to Order**: Oysters, obviously. Get a dozen raw to start. Then move on to the baked specialties. The "3-in-1" sampler gives you a taste of their Jalapeño/Bacon/Cheese, Garlic/Parmesan, and Cajun creations. Beyond oysters, the "El Dusty" fried fish sandwich is a massive, delicious mess, and the steamed shrimp are always a solid choice.
*   **Pro Tip**: There are three locations, but the original on Front Beach Road is the most iconic. Parking is tight and the place is always packed. The secret is to go at an off-time, like 2:30 PM for a late lunch. Or, just embrace the wait and consider it part of the charm.
*   **Address (Original)**: [16450 Front Beach Rd, Panama City Beach, FL 32413](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=16450+Front+Beach+Rd,+Panama+City+Beach,+FL+32413)

## The Best Place to Buy Seafood and Cook It Yourself

### Buddy's Seafood Market

Sometimes, the best seafood restaurant is your own kitchen. For those nights when you want to skip the crowds and enjoy a fresh seafood feast with a million-dollar view, there is no better place to provision than Buddy's. This isn't a restaurant; it's a market, and it's the gold standard for fresh, local seafood.

*   **Why Locals Love It**: The selection and the freshness are unmatched. You can see the shrimp boats from their docks. They carry everything from massive Gulf shrimp and fresh-shucked oysters to whatever the boats brought in that day—grouper, snapper, scamp, and more. Their house-made smoked tuna dip is dangerously addictive; buy the big container, you won't regret it.
*   **What to Order**: Ask the staff what's freshest—they'll never steer you wrong. The Royal Red shrimp, when they have them, are a must-buy. They're a deep-water shrimp with a sweet, lobster-like flavor. Grab a pound of those, a container of tuna dip, some cocktail sauce, and a key lime pie for the perfect beach-condo dinner.

## The Upscale Seafood Night Out

### Firefly

When you're ready to trade the flip-flops for something a little nicer and celebrate a special occasion, Firefly is the place. Famous for its stunning interior featuring a massive, illuminated tree in the center of the main dining room, the ambiance is matched by an equally impressive menu.

*   **Why Locals Love It**: It's where we go for anniversaries, birthdays, or just a really fantastic date night. While it's known for steaks and sushi, the kitchen's handle on fresh seafood is masterful. The service is impeccable, and the atmosphere feels like an escape from the typical beach town vibe.
*   **What to Order**: Start with the She-Crab soup. It's rich, creamy, and loaded with crab. The sushi is some of the best in the area, with creative rolls and incredibly fresh fish. For a main course, the Pan-Seared Grouper with a citrus beurre blanc is a consistent showstopper.
*   **Pro Tip**: Reservations are highly recommended, especially for a table in the main dining room under the tree. If you can't get one, the bar area (The "Library") is more casual, first-come-first-served, and offers the same incredible menu.
*   **Address**: [535 N Richard Jackson Blvd, Panama City Beach, FL 32407](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=535+N+Richard+Jackson+Blvd,+Panama+City+Beach,+FL+32407)

## The Casual Lunch Spot with a Killer View

### Schooners Last Local Beach Club

The motto here is "Lunch is a ceremony, Dinner is a tradition, and the Last Local Beach Club is a way of life." Schooners is one of the last remaining old-school, right-on-the-sand beach bars. It’s the perfect place to dig your toes in the sand, listen to live music, and enjoy a simple, perfectly executed seafood lunch.

![a beachside restaurant patio with people eating under umbrellas at sunset](/static/images/blog/panama-city-beach/a-beachside-restaurant-patio-with-people-eating-un-4bf34f.jpg)

*   **Why Locals Love It**: The location is unbeatable. It’s on the quieter, eastern end of the beach, and the entire "backyard" is the beach itself. It's incredibly family-friendly during the day and turns into a lively beach party at night. The daily sunset celebration, where they fire off an old cannon to salute the end of the day, is a cherished local tradition.
*   **What to Order**: Keep it classic. The Giant Gulf Grouper Sandwich (fried or grilled) is one of the best on the beach. The Seared Crab Cakes are packed with lump crab meat, not fillers. And the Tuna Medallions, served blackened with a wasabi drizzle, are a lighter option that doesn't skimp on flavor.
*   **Pro Tip**: Parking can be a nightmare. They have a small lot that fills up fast. Your best bet is to find a paid public spot nearby and walk, or even better, take a ride-share. Plan to be there an hour before sunset to grab a good spot for the cannon blast.
*   **Address**: [5121 Gulf Dr, Panama City, FL 32408](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=5121+Gulf+Dr,+Panama+City,+FL+32408)

## Frequently Asked Questions about PCB Seafood

### What is the best seafood to eat in Panama City Beach?
You're on the Gulf of Mexico, so you should focus on fresh, local catches. **Grouper** is the king of PCB fish—it's mild, flaky, and delicious fried, grilled, or blackened. **Gulf Shrimp**, especially Royal Reds, are sweet and succulent. And of course, **Oysters** from nearby Apalachicola Bay are a must-try, whether raw or baked.

### Is there a dress code for seafood restaurants in PCB?
For the most part, Panama City Beach is very casual. Places like Dusty's and Schooners are come-as-you-are (shorts and flip-flops are standard). For a more upscale experience like Firefly, you'll want to opt for "resort casual"—think collared shirts, nice shorts or pants, and sundresses.

### Where can I buy fresh seafood to cook in Panama City Beach?
The two undisputed local favorites are **Buddy's Seafood Market** and **Capt. Anderson's Waterfront Market**. Both offer a fantastic selection of fish, shrimp, oysters, and specialty items like smoked tuna dip that were likely caught that very same day.

### What's the best time to go to seafood restaurants in PCB to avoid crowds?
During the busy season (Spring Break through Labor Day), restaurants are packed. To beat the dinner rush, try to arrive between 4:00 PM and 5:00 PM. Alternatively, opt for a late lunch around 2:00 PM. The wait times between 6:00 PM and 8:00 PM can often exceed an hour at popular spots.
