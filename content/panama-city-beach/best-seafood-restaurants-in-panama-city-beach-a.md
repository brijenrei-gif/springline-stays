## The Grand Marlin: For Date Nights and Killer Views

If you’re looking for a slightly more upscale experience without the stuffiness, this is it. Perched right on the Grand Lagoon overlooking the marina, The Grand Marlin delivers on both ambiance and flavor. It’s my go-to for celebrating an anniversary or when I just want an incredible cocktail with a view.

### The Vibe
Coastal chic. You can get away with nice shorts, but you won't feel out of place if you decide to dress up a little. The main dining room is beautiful, but the real magic is at the North Drop Bar—an open-air bar with high-top tables and the best sunset views over the water.

### What to Order
The Grouper Piccata is legendary for a reason. It's perfectly pan-seared with a lemon-caper butter sauce that is just *chef's kiss*. If you're starting with appetizers, you can't go wrong with the Crispy Lobster Fingers.

### Pro Tip
You don't need a reservation for the North Drop Bar. If the main dining room is booked, head straight upstairs, grab a drink, and snag a table as soon as one opens up. The menu is the same, but the vibe is more relaxed.

## Schooners: The Last Local Beach Club

If you ask a local where to go for a casual, toes-in-the-sand meal, nine times out of ten, they’ll say Schooners. This place is a PCB institution located on the historic east end of the beach. It’s loud, it’s fun, and it’s unapologetically a beach bar.

### The Vibe
Pure, unadulterated beach energy. People walk up straight from the sand, there's always live music, and kids are busy playing volleyball while the adults relax. It's the definition of "come as you are."

### What to Order
Keep it simple. The Grouper Sandwich (grilled, never fried) is a classic for a reason. Their Seared Crab Cakes are also phenomenal—heavy on the crab, light on the filler, just as they should be.

### Pro Tip
Get there about an hour before sunset and find a spot on the main deck. Every evening at sunset, they fire off an old cannon to celebrate the end of another day in paradise. It's a little cheesy, but it's a tradition you have to experience at least once.

## Dusty's Oyster Bar: The Quintessential Dive

Don’t let the gritty exterior, the gravel parking lot, or the hundreds of signed dollar bills stapled to the ceiling fool you. Dusty's is a seafood mecca. This is where you go for cold beer and some of the best oysters in Florida.

### The Vibe
A true-blue, no-frills oyster bar. It’s cramped, it’s loud, and it’s perfect. It feels like a place that has a million stories to tell. It’s part biker bar, part family joint, and 100% authentic.

### What to Order
Oysters, obviously. The Baked Oysters are the star. Get the Dusty’s Dozen—a mix of their different baked creations. My personal favorite is the Stuffed Jalapeño with cream cheese, bacon, and a spicy kick. The Fried Shrimp Po' Boy is also a solid choice if you're not an oyster person.

### Pro Tip
There will be a wait, especially during peak season. There's no fancy reservation system. Just put your name on the list, grab a drink from the bar, and hang out in the parking lot. It's part of the experience.

## Captain Anderson's: A Timeless PCB Institution

Captain Anderson's is the OG of Panama City Beach dining. Open since 1967, this place is a well-oiled machine. It’s where your parents and grandparents probably ate when they visited. It’s not trendy, it’s not experimental—it's just consistently fantastic, classic Gulf Coast seafood.

### The Vibe
Old-school fine dining. You'll see families dressed for a nice dinner and fishing boat captains still in their deck shoes. The best seats in the house are by the massive windows overlooking the marina, where you can watch the fishing fleet unload their daily catch.

### What to Order
You can't go wrong with their "Famous" Grilled Bay Shrimp. They're cooked over a charcoal grill and have an incredible, smoky flavor. For a true delicacy, order the Scamp Imperial—a local fish baked with a rich crabmeat stuffing.

### Pro Tip
The secret to Captain Anderson's is to go early. Like, *be in line when the doors open* early. The restaurant seats over 700 people, and the wait can still get to be two hours long in the middle of summer.

## J. Michael's: The Hole-in-the-Wall Everyone Loves

Tucked away in a nondescript strip mall on Thomas Drive, J. Michael’s is the definition of a hidden gem. From the outside, you’d probably drive right by. Don't. Inside, you'll find some of the most delicious and creative seafood dishes in town.

### The Vibe
Cozy and unpretentious. It's a small, family-owned restaurant with a loyal local following. It feels more like a friendly neighborhood tavern than a beach-town restaurant.

### What to Order
The menu has a slight Greek influence. Order the Scamp St. Andrews—a gorgeous piece of fish topped with shrimp, scallops, and a creamy parmesan sauce. It sounds heavy, but it's perfectly balanced. The Blackened Oysters are also a must-try appetizer.

### Pro Tip
Because it's small, it fills up fast. They don't take reservations, so plan to go on a weeknight or get there right when they open to snag a table without a long wait.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most famous seafood restaurant in Panama City Beach?
Captain Anderson's is arguably the most famous and historic seafood restaurant in PCB. It has been a fine-dining landmark since 1967, known for its classic Gulf cuisine and for serving seafood fresh from its own fleet.

### Are there any casual, on-the-beach seafood restaurants?
Absolutely. Schooners is the best example, offering a true "toes-in-the-sand" dining experience with live music and a sunset cannon celebration. It's the perfect spot for a laid-back meal right on the Gulf.

### Do I need to make reservations for seafood restaurants in PCB?
For upscale places like The Grand Marlin, reservations are highly recommended, especially for weekend dinner service. For most casual spots like Dusty's Oyster Bar and Schooners, they do not take reservations, so it's best to arrive early or expect a wait during peak season.
