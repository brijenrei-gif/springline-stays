## The Unforgettable Waterfront Icons

These are the tried-and-true legends. They’ve been serving up incredible seafood for decades and have the waterfront views to match. You can't go wrong here.

### Captain Anderson's Restaurant & Waterfront Market
If there’s one "must-do" fine dining seafood experience in PCB, it’s Captain Anderson's. Situated on the Grand Lagoon, you can watch the fishing fleets roll in with their daily catch right from your table. It’s an institution, and for good reason. The menu is a mix of classic and contemporary, but their baked and broiled dishes are where they truly shine.

*   **Vibe:** Old-school, bustling, classic waterfront fine dining.
*   **What to Order:** The "Famous" Grilled Bay Shrimp is a rite of passage. For something truly special, get the Captain's Platter, broiled.
*   **Local's Pro-Tip:** The wait here can be legendary, especially in peak season. Arrive right when they open (around 4:30 PM) or plan to have a drink at the bar. Better yet, go on a weeknight to avoid the biggest crowds.

### Schooners Last Local Beach Club
"Breakfast, lunch, and dinner... and a sunset." That’s the motto at Schooners, and it’s spot on. This is the ultimate beach-on-your-toes restaurant. It’s ultra-casual, always has live music, and boasts one of the best sunset views in town. The food is straightforward, beachy, and delicious.

*   **Vibe:** Super casual, sandy, live music, party atmosphere.
*   **What to Order:** The Seared Crab Cakes are loaded with crab, not filler. The Tuna Medallions are also a local favorite.
*   **Local's Pro-Tip:** Time your visit for the daily sunset celebration. They fire off an old cannon every single night to mark the occasion. Get there at least an hour beforehand to snag a prime table on the sand.

## Casual Joints & Legendary Oyster Bars

Sometimes you just want to throw on a t-shirt and shorts, crack open some oysters, and drink a cold beer. These are the spots for that. No pretense, just fantastic seafood.

### Dusty's Oyster Bar
Look for the shack covered in dollar bills and you’ve found Dusty’s. This place is the definition of a dive, and we mean that in the best way possible. It’s loud, it’s crowded, and it serves some of the best oysters on the beach. If you're looking for white tablecloths, move along. If you're looking for character and amazing baked oysters, pull up a stool.

*   **Vibe:** Ultimate dive bar, loud, fun, and covered in signed dollar bills.
*   **What to Order:** Oysters, any way you can get them. The baked oysters with jalapeño, bacon, and cheese are a game-changer.
*   **Local's Pro-Tip:** The best seats in the house are right at the bar where you can watch the shuckers work their magic. Don't be shy—ask them for their recommendation of the day.

### Hunt's Oyster Bar & Seafood Restaurant
A short drive over the bridge into historic St. Andrews will land you at Hunt’s, another no-frills local legend. It’s a small, unassuming spot that's been a local secret for years (though the secret is getting out). They're famous for their Apalachicola oysters and Cajun-inspired dishes.

*   **Vibe:** Small, no-frills, diner-style, authentically local.
*   **What to Order:** A dozen raw oysters and a cup of their seafood gumbo. The Three-Cheese Cajun Baked Oysters will make you a believer.
*   **Local's Pro-Tip:** Hunt's is tiny and doesn't take reservations, so expect a wait. It's 100% worth it. Go for a late lunch or an early dinner on a weekday to minimize your time in line.

## For a Fancier Night Out

Celebrating an anniversary, a birthday, or just the fact that you're on vacation? These spots offer a more refined atmosphere without losing that coastal charm.

### The Grand Marlin Restaurant & Oyster Bar
With stunning views of the Grand Lagoon, The Grand Marlin is the perfect spot for a special occasion. The atmosphere is sophisticated but still relaxed and beachy. They print their menus daily to reflect the freshest catch, and their cocktail list is one of the best in the city.

*   **Vibe:** Upscale coastal, beautiful views, perfect for date night.
*   **What to Order:** Start with the Crispy Lobster Fingers. For the main, whatever the fresh catch of the day is, prepared "sautéed meunière style."
*   **Local's Pro-Tip:** Head upstairs to the North Drop Bar for a more casual experience with the same fantastic food and an even better view. It's a great spot to catch the sunset before dinner.

### Firefly
While not strictly a seafood restaurant, Firefly deserves a spot on this list for its incredible atmosphere and standout seafood dishes. You walk in and are immediately greeted by a massive, brightly-lit tree right in the middle of the dining room. It’s magical. The food lives up to the decor, with a focus on fresh, local ingredients.

*   **Vibe:** Enchanting, romantic, unique. Feels like a special event.
*   **What to Order:** The She-Crab Soup is famous for a reason. For an entree, the Sesame Seared Tuna or the Pan-Seared Grouper are always flawless.
*   **Local's Pro-Tip:** Firefly has an excellent happy hour in their library-themed bar area. It's the perfect way to experience the ambiance and a few appetizers without committing to a full, formal dinner. Reservations are highly recommended for the main dining room.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most famous seafood restaurant in Panama City Beach?
Hands down, **Captain Anderson's Restaurant** is the most famous and historic seafood spot in PCB. It's been a family-owned institution for over 50 years and is known for its classic dishes and views of the fishing fleet.

### What is the best casual seafood restaurant in PCB?
For a truly casual, "sand-between-your-toes" experience, **Schooners Last Local Beach Club** is the top choice. For a legendary dive bar feel with amazing oysters, you can't beat **Dusty's Oyster Bar**.

### Do I need reservations for seafood restaurants in Panama City Beach?
During peak season (Spring Break and summer), reservations are highly recommended for popular upscale spots like **The Grand Marlin** and **Firefly**. For most casual places like Schooners or Dusty's, they are first-come, first-served, so be prepared for a wait during prime dinner hours.
