## The Unforgettable Waterfront Experience
<a id="waterfront-experience"></a>

There's nothing better than a sea breeze and a plate of fresh seafood. These are the go-to spots when you want dinner with a world-class view.

### Schooners Last Local Beach Club

**The Vibe:** It doesn't get more "PCB" than Schooners. This is the ultimate beach bar and restaurant, with tables spilling right out onto the sand. It's laid-back, loud, and pure fun. Go for the sunset—they fire a cannon every evening to celebrate.

**Must-Order:** The Seared Tuna Steak Sandwich is a classic, but my personal favorite is the Grouper Medallions. They're lightly fried and perfect for sharing (or not).

**Local Tip:** Check the sunset time online and plan to arrive at least an hour beforehand to snag a prime beachside table. The parking lot fills up fast, so consider a rideshare or be prepared for a short walk.

### The Grand Marlin

**The Vibe:** Upscale but not stuffy, The Grand Marlin is where you go for a refined waterfront meal with stunning views of Grand Lagoon. It's perfect for a date night or a special occasion.

**Must-Order:** The Grouper Piccata is a showstopper. Perfectly cooked fish with a tangy lemon-caper sauce that's just incredible. Also, don't sleep on their selection of fresh-shucked oysters from around the country.

**Local Tip:** Request a table on the upstairs covered deck when you make your reservation. The view is unbeatable, especially as the fishing charters roll back into the marina at dusk.

## Casual Joints for Oysters & a Cold Beer
<a id="casual-joints"></a>

Sometimes you just want to throw on a t-shirt, knock back some oysters, and not worry about making a reservation. These spots are your answer.

### Dusty's Oyster Bar

**The Vibe:** If you see a building covered in dollar bills, you're in the right place. Dusty's is a legendary dive bar that serves some of the best oysters and coldest beer on the beach. It's small, always packed, and has character for days.

**Must-Order:** You have to get the baked oysters. My go-to is the "Dusty's Dozen," which gives you a sample of their best-baked creations, including a jalapeño-cheddar combo that is out of this world.

**Local Tip:** This place is tiny, so expect a wait. The secret is to go at an "off" time, like 3 PM on a Tuesday. Also, while they take cards now, it's always felt like a "bring cash" kind of place.

### Hunt's Oyster Bar & Seafood Restaurant

**The Vibe:** Located just over the bridge in St. Andrews, Hunt's is a no-frills institution. This is where the actual fishermen eat. The decor hasn't changed in decades, and that's exactly why people love it.

**Must-Order:** The Three-Dozen Steamed Oysters with a side of garlic butter. They come out piping hot on a huge tray, and it's a true feast. Their fried shrimp is also top-tier—lightly battered and perfectly seasoned.

**Local Tip:** Hunt's gets insanely busy. Your best bet is to go for a late lunch. If you see a line out the door, put your name on the list and grab a drink at one of the nearby spots in historic St. Andrews while you wait.

## For a Special Night Out
<a id="special-night"></a>

When you're ready to celebrate and want an elevated dining experience, these are the tables to book.

### Captain Anderson's Restaurant

**The Vibe:** A Panama City Beach landmark since 1967. Captain Anderson's is an old-school, family-friendly fine dining establishment overlooking the Grand Lagoon. You can watch the fleet unload their daily catch right from your table. It's a classic for a reason.

**Must-Order:** The "Famous" Grilled Bay Shrimp. They're cooked in an incredible garlic butter sauce over a charcoal grill. It's simple, perfect, and what they're known for. For a true splurge, the "Capt. Anderson's Imperial" is a crab-stuffed flounder that will change your life.

**Local Tip:** Get there *before* 5:00 PM, especially during peak season. Seriously. The wait can be legendary. If you do get stuck waiting, their bar is a comfortable spot to grab a classic cocktail.

### Firefly

**The Vibe:** Tucked away in a shopping center, Firefly is a hidden gem. You walk in, and you're transported. The main dining room features a huge, illuminated tree in the center, creating a magical, romantic atmosphere.

**Must-Order:** While known for their steaks, the seafood here is exceptional. The Pan-Seared Scallops are always cooked to perfection. For something unique, try the She-Crab Soup—it's rich, creamy, and one of the best you'll ever have.

**Local Tip:** Firefly has an excellent happy hour in their library-themed bar. It's a great way to experience the high-end food and cocktails at a more approachable price point.

## Frequently Asked Questions
<a id="faq"></a>

### What is the most famous seafood restaurant in Panama City Beach?
Captain Anderson's is arguably the most famous and historic seafood restaurant in PCB. It's been a family tradition for generations of visitors, known for its classic dishes and views of the fishing fleet.

### Are there any casual, beachfront seafood restaurants in PCB?
Absolutely. Schooners Last Local Beach Club is the quintessential casual beachfront spot where you can eat with your toes in the sand. It’s perfect for a laid-back lunch or a sunset dinner.

### Do I need to make reservations for seafood restaurants in Panama City Beach?
It depends. For upscale places like The Grand Marlin or Firefly, reservations are highly recommended, especially on weekends or during peak season. For casual spots like Dusty's or Hunt's, they often don't take reservations, so you should plan for a potential wait.
